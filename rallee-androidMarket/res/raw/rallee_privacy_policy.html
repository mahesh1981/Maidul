4. WHAT ARE COOKIES AND GIFS HOW DOES RALLEE USE THEM?

Key Points

By using Rallee’s websites and/or software you are giving your consent to the use of cookies. You can choose to reject cookies at any time, either by changing your browser settings (if your browser permits) or by ceasing to use Rallee’s websites and/or software or by using the applicable opt-out functions. You should be aware that certain features are only available through the use of cookies and if you choose not to accept cookies, such features may not be available to you.

4.1 What are Cookies and GIFs?

4.1.1. Cookies

Cookies are small files containing a string of characters (text) that are sent to your browser from a website’s server. The cookie may contain a unique identifier but it does not contain personally identifiable information such as your name or email address. Rallee may use cookies when you visit its websites, or use Rallee’s software. The browser of the Rallee software client or your Internet browser stores the cookie on your computer’s hard drive and this may be accessed by the website during your next visit. Other websites may also send cookies to your browser but your browser will not allow those websites to see Rallee’s cookie data.

4.1.2. Gifs

Rallee web pages and client interfaces may include clear gifs, also known as web-beacons and tracking pixels. Gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, which are used to count your visits to the page (but which do not retain any personally identifiable information). In addition, Rallee uses clear gifs in our HTML-based emails to let us know which emails have been opened and acted upon. This allows us to gauge the effectiveness of certain communications and the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns

4.4 Third Party Cookies

In addition to Rallee’s cookies, Rallee’s analytics and affiliate partners may set cookies and access cookies on your computer, when you are using the Rallee software client or visiting a Rallee website.

4.4.1 Analytics

Rallee uses analytics partners Google Analytics to gather anonymous statistical and analytical information about how users use Rallee websites and software clients. For example, these partners gather and aggregate data on page views and clicks on our sites, the search engines and keywords used to find our sites, links accessed from our sites, error and crash information about our clients, and similar information. These analytics are not used to track your journey to other websites or to identify you.

4.4.2. Affiliates

Rallee’s affiliate network program partner allows third parties to introduce people to Rallee. Our affiliates may send cookies to your computer to allow them to receive information (a) when you click on a Rallee related link on one of our affiliate partner sites, and/or (b) when you make a purchase in Rallee’s software, for the purpose of paying commission to the affiliate partner. If you re-visit Rallee’s site directly, our affiliate network program partner can recognize you and reward the third-party affiliate partner site that referred you to our site. These cookies are not within Rallee’s control.

4.4.3. Other Third Party Content

Sometimes, Rallee’s websites may include widgets. When you view this widget, you may be accepting cookies from these websites. These cookies are not within our control and you should check with the relevant third party site for more information.

4.5 Your Choices about Cookies

We don’t recommend that you restrict or block cookies as this may impact on the functionality of our websites and products. However, if you choose to do so, please see the options available to you below. You can also find comprehensive information about cookies atwww.aboutcookies.org.

4.5.1 Browser Cookies

The Help option on the toolbar of most browsers will tell you how to prevent your browser from accepting new cookies, how to have the browser notify you when you receive a new cookie, how to delete cookies or how to disable cookies altogether.
